Referendum in Taiwan





Referendum Background





The referendum was scheduled after judges deemed the current legislation towards same-sex marriage as un-democratic. The lawmakers gave the Taiwanese government two years to enact legislative reform to allow same-sex marriage. The Taiwanese Government scheduled a referendum to put same-sex marraige to the vote. Taiwan was hopeful that the referendum result would pave the way for representing the first country in Asia to allow same-sex marriage. However, when put to the people, this was voted against. This represented a massive defeat for the LGBT+ community in Taiwan.

For & Against

Supporters

  • Taiwan's first female president was elected in 2016, Tsai Ing-wen. She announced her support for same-sex marriage.
  • Tongzhi Hotline Association was founded in 1998 and has had a profound influence on promoting LGBT+ rights.

Defenders

  • Family Guardian Coalition consists of many of Taiwan's Christian denominations. It was organised in 2013 to pressure legislators against the proposed amendments to the civil code to legalise same-sex marriage.
