Activists





Zhang Huimei

Zhang Huimei is better known by her nickname A-mei. She is a Taiwanese Puyuma singer and songwriter. She uses her popularity to help the LGBT+ community by organising fundraising campaigns.



Chi Chia Wei

Chi Chia Wei is a Taiwanese civil rights activist. In 1986, he became the first person in Taiwan to come out as gay on national TV, organising a press conference to announce his sexuality and launch of a campaign to prevent the spread of AIDS/HIV.



Cai Yilin

Cai Yilin is a Taiwanese singer, songwriter, dancer, actress as well as business woman. She has a dedicated fanbase composed mostly of women and LGBT+ people. In 2007, Tasi's "Bravo Lover" became the theme song of the 2007 Taiwan Pride parade. Additionally, the majority of her songs cater to an LGBT+ audience and support same-sex love.



Ho Chuen-juei

Ho Chuen-juei goes by her nickmane, Josephine Ho. She is the chair of the English department of the National Central University, Taiwan, and the coordinartor of its Center For the Study of Sexualities. She is one of the most well-known feminist scholars in Taiwan and is also called "the godmother of the Taiwanese queer movement".



Yu Mei-nu

Yu Mei-nu is a Taiwanses politician. She is a staunch supporter for the legalisation of same-sex marriage in Tawian, and known for her advocacy of women's rights.



Zhu Huiliang

Zhu Huiliang goes by her nickname, Josephine Chu. She is a Taiwanese former politician. She has worked to expand LGBT+ rights in Taiwan as well as having advocated for the rights of foreign spouses.
